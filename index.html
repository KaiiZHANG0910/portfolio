Integrated Studio Project 2025

EDIT: A Dialogue with
Digital Memory

The inspiration for "EDIT" comes from my personal diary. As a girl from Shanghai studying in Hong Kong, browsing through my diary often makes my past memories and the people I cherish feel like a distant dream. The physical distance creates a sense of unreality, leading me to a haunting question: What if this world, including my memory and experience, is entirely coded by others?

This existential doubt was amplified after I experienced a scam in Hong Kong. It was an alarming wake-up call that forced me to confront the blurred lines of reality in our AI age.

"What is truly real in this AI age? What if the messages, people, and experiences can all be generated and coded?"

While this initial thought was desperate and hopeless, I found my own answer: Emotions, reactions, and feelings are not fake. They flow through all modes of communication. Just as people in ancient times waited ages for letters, technology today helps us reach those we love, carrying the same weight of emotion.

Visual Language

The video begins by questioning "realness." Visually, I displayed raw code to create an unreal, Cyberpunk atmosphere. These lines were written in Java syntax, yet they are intentionally "fake"—a metaphor for the constructed nature of digital life.

In contrast, the second half of the piece is densely layered with sound, incorporating authentic voice recordings from my mother and grandmother. This juxtaposition aims to create a pure sensory scene, allowing the audience to experience the undeniable reality of love within the soundwaves.

Live Performance

Questioning

This performance combines projection installations, dance, and motion graphics to construct the mental landscape of a patient with bipolar disorder. I chose this theme because, unlike depression, bipolar disorder remains widely misunderstood.

Patients experience two violent extremes: depressive lows with oppressed energy, and manic highs with uncontrollable emotion. Like water and fire, cold and warm, stillness and motion—from an artistic perspective, this condition possesses powerful contrast and dramatic tension.

"Like water and fire... from an artistic perspective, it possesses strong contrast and dramatic tension."
The Narrative

Unpredictable emotional switching often confuses those unfamiliar with the condition, leading to stigma. Furthermore, seeking help is not as simple as visiting a psychologist; the act itself requires overcoming enormous psychological barriers.

I designed a storyline where a girl with bipolar disorder engages in a dialogue with a doctor. However, the doctor's endless questioning pushes her mental state into a more fragile condition.

Technical Execution

To emphasize the alienation, the doctor's voice was processed to mimic an AI tone—emotionless and cold. During the performance, the actor playing the doctor appeared in a white coat via a live camera feed. His image was projected onto arranged physical blocks using MadMapper to simulate a distorted, old television effect, symbolizing the disconnect between the patient and the clinical system.
